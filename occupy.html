Occupy DC

Inspired by the first artwork that emerged from the Occupy Wall Street movement, I noticed that Washington, DC had gone unrepresented and was inspired to contribute in some way. The colors of this work were influenced by Perestroika movement propaganda, displaying a statistical message in the typographic form of the National Monument. The "1%" is accentuated at the apex of the monument, while the "99%" is represented at its base.

Assessing the Art of
the Occupy Movement

After submitting my work to the Occuprint website in late November 2011, The Washington Post contacted me and asked my permission to printed my work in the December 6, 2011 issue within the article "Nothing Shy About the Art of Occupy": Assessing the Art of the Occupy Movement. The following spring, Occuprint selected 30 pieces out of hundreds of submissions and opted to include Occupy DC in its official portfolio. The Occuprint portfolio was showcased in New York City and there are current exhibits in universities across the nation.
